The Waiting Queue

The bot manages the waiting queue that tracks members that are waiting to join a boat on one of our fleets. Members may join the queue by clicking on the “join queue” button in the waiting queue message. This is different from how the bot was run previously (in Fleets of Fortune) where simply joining the waiting room would add you to the queue.

The Waiting Queue

The picture above shows the queue message, which is posted in the fleet-queue channel.

When a user clicks on the Join Queue button, they will see themselves added to the queue after a short delay. If they are already in the queue, they will be sent an ephemeral message notifying them that they are already in the queue.

When a user clicks on the Leave Queue button, the queue message will be updated to remove them from the queue. If the user is not in the queue, they will be sent an ephemeral message telling them they are not in the queue.

A user may specify their desired activity and/or emissary by interacting with either of the dropdowns. When they do this, if they are in the queue, their entry will be updated with their desired activity and/or emissary, defaulting to Any/Anything.

 

When a member joins a fleet voice channel

Whenever a member joins a voice channel that is in one of the categories marked by “SoT Fleet [N]”, the bot checks if they are in the queue. If they are, the bot sends a message asking if they successfully joined the boat, and starts an internal countdown. If and only if they respond to the bot’s message before the countdown has expired (countdown time is currently 2 minutes) saying they did not successfully join the ship, they will retain their position in the queue. Otherwise, they will be removed from the queue once the countdown ends.

 

Commands

All of the commands for managing the queue are subcommands under the /waiting-queue command.

  • /waiting-queue reset - Resends the queue message (useful if the message appears to be buggy). This will preserve all of the members in the queue.

  • /waiting-queue clear This empties the queue of all members, but does not delete or resend the queue message.

  • /waiting-queue remove-user [user] - Removes the specified user from the queue.

  • /waiting-queue add-user [user] - Adds the specified user to the end of the queue.

 

Queue-Adjacent Commands

  • /sotinvite [user] [fleet-number] [ship-number] - Sends a message to the user (via both a DM and a message mentioning them in the invites channel) telling them to join the channel (channel will be clickable, allowing them to join by clicking on that message).

Fleet Management

The bot helps staff manage the currently active fleets via several commands that manipulate the fleet channels. For the most part, the role the bot plays in managing the fleet is just through commands that are issued by staff. However, the bot also manages the permissions for the fleet-chat text channels for each of the fleets.

 

Commands

  • /create-sota - Creates a new SoT fleet category channel as well as a role (if it didn’t exist) for that fleet. The role is named Fleet-N where N is the number of the fleet that was created.

  • /rename-sota [old] [new] - Renames the fleet category channel to a new number (e.g. from SoT Fleet 2 to SoT Fleet 1)

  • /remove-sota [number] - Removes the fleet category and channels within it. Also removes the Fleet-Number role

 

Definitions and Terms

Ephemeral message - A message that is only seen by the user. Only the user to whom the message is sent may delete it (the bot cannot).
